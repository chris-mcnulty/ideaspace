## Getting Started

### What is Nebula?

Nebula is a collaborative envisioning platform that helps teams ideate, categorize, vote, and prioritize ideas together. Whether you're running a product vision workshop, strategic planning session, or innovation challenge, Nebula provides the structure and tools to guide your team through the process.

### How It Works

1. **Ideation** - Participants contribute ideas on a digital whiteboard
2. **Categorization** - AI and facilitators organize ideas into themes
3. **Voting** - Multiple voting mechanisms (pairwise, ranking, marketplace) help prioritize
4. **Results** - AI-powered summaries and personalized participant insights

### Account Types

- **Guest Access** - Join sessions without creating an account using a generated alias
- **Registered User** - Create an account for full features and personalized results
- **Facilitator** - Run and manage envisioning sessions
- **Company Admin** - Manage your organization's workspaces and users
- **Global Admin** - Platform-wide administration and configuration

## For Participants

### Joining a Session

#### As a Guest
1. Receive a workspace code from your facilitator (format: `1234-5678`)
2. Visit the platform and enter the workspace code
3. Choose "Join as Guest"
4. A creative alias is automatically generated (e.g., "Brave Phoenix")
5. Click "Join" to enter the workspace

#### As a Registered User
1. Log in to your Nebula account
2. Enter the workspace code or click on a shared link
3. Click "Join Workspace"
4. You'll be identified by your registered name

### Contributing Ideas

When the **Ideation Phase** is active:

1. Click the **"Add Idea"** button
2. Enter your idea in the text field
3. Keep ideas concise and clear (1-2 sentences works best)
4. Click **"Submit"** to add your idea to the whiteboard
5. Your idea appears instantly for all participants to see

**Tips for Great Ideas:**
- Be specific and actionable
- Focus on one idea per submission
- Build on others' ideas by referencing them
- Use clear language everyone can understand

### Viewing Ideas

- All ideas appear as cards on the digital whiteboard
- Ideas are color-coded by category (once categorized)
- You can see who submitted each idea (or "Anonymous" for guests)
- Ideas update in real-time as others contribute

### Pairwise Voting

When the **Voting Phase** begins:

1. You'll see two idea cards side-by-side
2. Read both ideas carefully
3. Click on the idea you prefer
4. The next pair appears automatically
5. Continue until you've voted on all pairs

**What you're evaluating:**
- Which idea better addresses the session's purpose
- Which idea is more feasible or impactful
- Your personal preference based on the criteria

**Tips:**
- Take your time with each decision
- You can't go back, so consider each pair carefully
- There are no wrong answers - vote based on your judgment

### Stack Ranking

When the **Ranking Phase** is active:

1. You'll see a list of ideas
2. Drag and drop to reorder them
3. Place your most preferred idea at the top
4. Place your least preferred idea at the bottom
5. Click **"Submit Ranking"** when satisfied

**Ranking Strategy:**
- Consider the session's stated goals
- Balance impact, feasibility, and novelty
- Your top choices carry more weight in the final results

### Marketplace Allocation

When the **Marketplace Phase** opens:

1. You receive a budget of virtual coins (typically 100)
2. Browse the ideas presented
3. Click **"+"** to allocate coins to ideas you support
4. Click **"-"** to remove coins
5. Your remaining budget is shown at the top
6. Allocate all or some of your budget as you see fit

**Allocation Tips:**
- You can put all coins on one idea or spread them across many
- Ideas you allocate more coins to show stronger support
- You can change your allocation until the phase ends
- Watch the leaderboard to see community preferences

### Viewing Results

When the workspace closes:

1. Access the **Results** page via the workspace link
2. View the **Cohort Summary** with top themes and ideas
3. See voting results across all mechanisms
4. Download your **Personalized Results** PDF with:
   - Your alignment score with the group
   - Your top contributions
   - Personalized recommendations
   - AI-generated insights based on your participation

## For Facilitators

### Creating a Workspace

1. Navigate to your **Dashboard**
2. Click **"Create Workspace"**
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Name** - Descriptive title (e.g., "Product Vision 2025")
   - **Purpose** - Clear goal statement for participants
   - **Organization** - Select your organization
   - **Guest Access** - Enable to allow anonymous participation
4. Click **"Create"**
5. Your workspace receives a unique 8-digit code

### Configuring Your Workspace

#### Opening a Workspace

1. Go to your workspace
2. Click **"Open Workspace"**
3. The status changes to "Open" and participants can join

#### Session Timing

**Live Mode**: All phases are manually controlled by you
**Async Mode**: Set specific date/time windows for each phase

To configure timing:
1. Go to the **Settings** tab
2. Set start and end times for:
   - Ideation Phase
   - Voting Phase
   - Ranking Phase
   - Marketplace Phase

#### Sharing Access

**Share Links:**
1. Click **"Share Links"** button in your workspace
2. Copy links for specific sections:
   - Waiting Room (participant join page)
   - Ideation/Whiteboard
   - Pairwise Voting
   - Stack Ranking
   - Marketplace
   - Results
3. Generate QR codes for in-person sessions

**Workspace Code:**
- Found at the top of your workspace (format: `1234-5678`)
- Participants enter this code to join
- Works for all access points

### Managing Ideas

#### Viewing Submissions

1. Go to the **Whiteboard** tab
2. All participant ideas appear as cards
3. Real-time updates as ideas are submitted

#### Editing Ideas

1. Click on any idea card
2. Click the **edit icon**
3. Make necessary changes
4. Click **"Save"**

**Use cases:**
- Fix typos or formatting
- Clarify vague submissions
- Remove inappropriate content

#### Merging Ideas

When participants submit duplicate or similar ideas:

1. Select multiple idea cards (checkbox in corner)
2. Click **"Merge Selected"**
3. Review the ideas to be merged
4. Edit the merged idea text
5. Click **"Merge"**
6. The duplicate ideas are combined into one

#### Deleting Ideas

1. Click on an idea card
2. Click the **delete icon**
3. Confirm deletion
4. The idea is permanently removed

### AI-Powered Categorization

#### Running AI Categorization

1. Go to the **Categories** tab
2. Click **"AI Categorize Ideas"**
3. The AI analyzes all ideas and suggests themes
4. Categories appear with color coding
5. Ideas are automatically assigned to categories

**How it works:**
- Uses OpenAI GPT-4o to analyze semantic similarities
- Creates 3-7 categories based on natural themes
- Assigns each idea to the most relevant category
- Provides category descriptions

#### Manual Categorization

1. Create a new category:
   - Click **"Add Category"**
   - Enter name and choose a color
   - Click **"Save"**

2. Assign ideas to categories:
   - Drag and drop ideas into category sections
   - Or click an idea and select its category

3. Edit categories:
   - Click the edit icon on any category
   - Rename or change color
   - Click **"Save"**

#### Category Override

After AI categorization:
1. Review suggested categories
2. Rename categories to better fit your context
3. Move ideas between categories as needed
4. Add new categories if the AI missed a theme
5. Your changes override the AI suggestions

### Knowledge Base Integration

The Knowledge Base grounds AI responses in your organization's documents.

#### Adding Documents

1. Go to the **Knowledge Base** tab
2. Click **"Upload Document"**
3. Select scope:
   - **Workspace** - Only this workspace
   - **Organization** - All workspaces in your org
   - **System** - Platform-wide (Global Admins only)
4. Upload files (PDF, DOCX, TXT, MD, CSV)
5. Add title and description
6. Click **"Upload"**

**Supported formats:**
- PDF documents
- Word documents (.docx)
- Text files (.txt)
- Markdown files (.md)
- CSV spreadsheets

#### Using Knowledge Base

When AI features are used (categorization, rewrites, results generation):
- AI automatically references relevant uploaded documents
- Ensures AI responses align with your organization's context
- Provides more accurate and relevant suggestions

### Managing Voting Phases

#### Pairwise Voting

1. Ensure ideas are submitted
2. Open the **Voting** phase via timing settings
3. Monitor participation in the **Analytics** tab
4. View the real-time leaderboard showing current rankings

#### Stack Ranking

1. Open the **Ranking** phase
2. Participants see the full list of ideas
3. Results use Borda Count scoring
4. Higher rankings give ideas more points

#### Marketplace Allocation

1. Set the coin budget (default: 100 coins per participant)
2. Open the **Marketplace** phase
3. Participants allocate coins to their preferred ideas
4. View the leaderboard showing total coins per idea

### Generating Results

#### Cohort Summary

1. Close your workspace when voting is complete
2. Go to the **Results** tab
3. Click **"Generate Cohort Summary"**
4. AI creates a comprehensive report including:
   - Key themes identified
   - Top-ranked ideas with explanations
   - Voting insights and patterns
   - Recommendations for next steps

#### Personalized Results

1. Click **"Generate Personalized Results"**
2. AI creates custom reports for each participant
3. Each report includes:
   - Alignment score with group preferences
   - Their top contributions
   - Personalized recommendations
   - Ideas that match their preferences

#### Exporting Results

**PDF Export:**
1. Click **"Export as PDF"**
2. Select report type (Cohort or Personalized)
3. PDF is generated with your organization's branding
4. Download or share with stakeholders

**CSV Export:**
1. Go to **Data Export** tab
2. Select what to export:
   - Ideas with categories
   - Voting results
   - Ranking results
   - Marketplace allocations
3. Click **"Export to CSV"**
4. Open in Excel or other tools for analysis

### Workspace Templates

#### Saving a Workspace as Template

When you've configured a workspace you want to reuse:

1. Go to your workspace
2. Click **"Save as Template"**
3. Choose scope:
   - **Organization Template** - Reusable within your org
   - **System Template** - Platform-wide (Global Admins only)
4. Add a descriptive name
5. Click **"Save"**

**What gets saved:**
- Workspace configuration and settings
- Categories (if already created)
- Phase timing templates
- Knowledge base document references
- Note: Ideas are NOT saved in templates

#### Using Templates

When creating a new workspace:
1. Click **"Create from Template"**
2. Browse available templates
3. Select a template
4. Customize the name and settings
5. Click **"Create"**
6. Your new workspace has the template's structure

### Closing Workspaces

#### Manual Close

1. Go to your workspace
2. Click **"Close Workspace"**
3. Confirm the action
4. Status changes to "Closed"
5. Participants can no longer submit or vote (unless results are made public)

#### Results Access After Closure

1. Go to **Results** tab
2. Find the **"Results Access After Workspace Closure"** card
3. Toggle to **"Make Accessible"**
4. Participants can view results even after workspace closes
5. Toggle to **"Restrict Access"** to limit access to facilitators/admins only

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### "Workspace Not Found"

**Cause:** Invalid workspace code or workspace deleted
**Solution:**
- Double-check the workspace code format (`1234-5678`)
- Verify with your facilitator that the workspace still exists
- Ensure you're on the correct organization URL

#### "Guest Access Disabled"

**Cause:** Workspace doesn't allow guest participants
**Solution:**
- Register for an account
- Contact the facilitator to enable guest access
- Use the "Request Access" feature if available

#### "Phase Not Active"

**Cause:** The current phase hasn't started yet or has ended
**Solution:**
- Check the workspace timing for when phases open
- Contact your facilitator if you believe this is an error
- Wait for the facilitator to open the next phase

#### Ideas Not Appearing

**Cause:** WebSocket connection issue or browser problem
**Solution:**
- Refresh the page
- Check your internet connection
- Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari recommended)
- Clear browser cache and cookies

#### Can't Submit Idea

**Cause:** Ideation phase closed or network issue
**Solution:**
- Verify ideation phase is active (look for green "Active" indicator)
- Check that you haven't exceeded any submission limits
- Ensure you're properly joined to the workspace
- Try refreshing the page

#### PDF Export Fails

**Cause:** Large dataset or browser memory issue
**Solution:**
- Try exporting in a different browser
- Contact your facilitator to export on your behalf
- Try CSV export as an alternative
- Close other browser tabs to free up memory

## Glossary

**Workspace** - A collaborative session environment where teams ideate and vote on ideas

**Workspace Code** - 8-digit identifier (format: `1234-5678`) used to join a workspace

**Ideation** - The phase where participants submit ideas

**Categorization** - Grouping ideas into themes or topics

**Pairwise Voting** - Voting mechanism where users compare two ideas at a time

**Stack Ranking** - Ordering all ideas from most to least preferred

**Marketplace Allocation** - Voting by distributing a budget of virtual coins across ideas

**Cohort Summary** - AI-generated report of the group's collective insights

**Personalized Results** - Individual participant report with alignment scores and recommendations

**Knowledge Base** - Collection of documents used to ground AI responses

**Template** - Reusable workspace configuration saved for future sessions

**Guest** - Participant without a registered account

**Facilitator** - User who creates and manages workspace sessions

**Company Admin** - User who manages an organization's settings and users

**Global Admin** - Platform administrator with full system access

## Security & Privacy

### Data Security

- All passwords are encrypted using bcrypt
- Sessions use secure HTTP-only cookies
- Database connections are encrypted
- Multi-tenant architecture ensures organization data isolation

### Privacy

- Guest participants can use generated aliases for anonymity
- Email addresses are only used for authentication and notifications
- Workspace data is only visible to organization members
- Results can be configured as public or private per workspace

### Access Control

- Role-based permissions at multiple levels
- Workspace-level access restrictions
- Guest access can be enabled/disabled per workspace
- Results visibility controlled by facilitators
